# WEB DESIGN & DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT

**("Agreement")**

- **{{client_name}}**, contact: **{{client_email}}** ("Client")
- **LuxWeb Studio**, contact: support@luxwebstudio.dev ("Contractor")

## 1. Scope & Deliverables

### 1.1 Deliverables

Contractor will design and develop a custom website (the "Project") that includes:

- Up to **{{pages_count}}** pages
- Custom design with two (2) revision rounds per phase (one revision round is a single collection of exchanges submitted at one time)
- Migration of existing page content
- Industry-standard security hardening
- Conversion-rate optimization best practices
- Three (3) months of post-launch support (the "Support Period")

The specific deliverables, timeline, and pricing described in the Proposal dated **{{proposal_date}}** are incorporated into this Agreement by reference as **Exhibit A**. In the event of any conflict between the Proposal and this Agreement, this Agreement governs.

### 1.2 Out of Scope / Ongoing Maintenance

After the Support Period ends, any additional work—new pages, change requests, DNS help, etc.—will be billed at **$100 per hour, Net 7**, subject to prior written approval by Client.

### 1.3 What's Not Included

- Ongoing hosting fees
- Domain registration or renewal
- Content creation (text, images, videos)
- Email setup or configuration
- E-commerce functionality (unless specifically contracted)
- Advanced custom development beyond scope
- SEO work beyond basic metadata
- ADA / WCAG compliance audits, remediation, or ongoing compliance

### 1.4 Expenses

Client will reimburse pre-approved expenses, including:

- Premium plugins or themes
- Stock photography licenses
- Third-party service setup fees

Contractor may incur individual expenses up to $150 without prior written approval; anything above that threshold requires written approval from Client before Contractor incurs the cost.

### 1.5 Change Orders

Any material change to the scope, deliverables, timeline, or price must be documented in a written change order signed (including by email confirmation) by both parties. Contractor will provide a written estimate of cost and timeline impact before the change order is approved. No work outside the original scope will be performed (and no additional charges will apply) until the change order is approved in writing.

### 1.6 Bug vs. Enhancement

For purposes of post-launch Support Period obligations, a "bug" is a defect where the delivered site does not function as specified in the designs, specifications, and scope approved by Client during Phases 1 and 2. Requests for new features, design changes, content changes, or functionality not specified in those approved deliverables are "enhancements" and are billable at the rate in § 1.2.

Contractor will issue a "ready-for-review" notice at the end of each phase. ## 5. Client Responsibilities

- Supply all logos, copy, images, and credentials within three (3) business days of Contractor's request, unless otherwise agreed. Failure to supply items and unresponsiveness may pause the Project, and a restart fee (see below) may apply.
- Provide consolidated, specific, and actionable feedback in writing within three (3) business days of each revision round. Missing any deadline may pause the Project, and a restart fee may apply. A paused Project will resume on a new schedule based on Contractor's then-current availability.
- Ensure all Client-supplied materials are properly licensed and non-infringing.
- Secure hosting, domain, and SSL certificates unless otherwise agreed.
- Maintain backups and ongoing security monitoring after the Support Period ends.

**Restart Fee:** If the Project is paused for more than fourteen (14) consecutive days due to Client delay or unresponsiveness, Contractor may charge a restart fee equal to twenty percent (20%) of the total Project Investment to reschedule and resume work. This fee compensates Contractor for the cost of re-prioritizing the Project and does not apply to delays caused by Contractor or by events under § 15 (Force Majeure).

## 7. Intellectual Property

### 7.1 Definitions

- **Work Product** means the final site files, custom code, designs, and content created specifically for Client under this Agreement.
- **Background IP** means pre-existing libraries, code snippets, frameworks, tools, techniques, and know-how that Contractor owns or is licensed to use and that Contractor incorporates into Work Product for multiple clients.
- **Open-Source Components** means any third-party software governed by an open-source or other third-party license (e.g., MIT, Apache 2.0, GPL, BSD).

### 7.2 Ownership

Upon receipt of final payment in full:

- Work Product is assigned to Client.
- Contractor retains all Background IP and grants Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free license to use the Background IP only as embedded in the Work Product.
- Open-Source Components are licensed to Client directly under their respective open-source licenses and are not assigned or sublicensed under this Agreement. Client is responsible for complying with those open-source license terms.
- Client permits Contractor to reuse non-confidential, generic code snippets (e.g., helper functions) in future projects, provided no Client trade secrets or confidential information are disclosed.

### 7.3 Source Code Delivery

Within seven (7) business days of final payment, Contractor will deliver Work Product source code to Client via (a) transfer of a GitHub repository to Client's GitHub account, or (b) a compressed archive (.zip) delivered via secure download link, at Client's election.

### 7.4 Portfolio Rights

Client grants Contractor the right to display non-confidential screenshots and a brief description of the Project in Contractor's portfolio, marketing materials, and social media. Contractor may include a discreet footer credit link to Contractor's website, which Client may request removal of in writing at any time after launch; Contractor will remove the footer credit within seven (7) business days of such request.

## 10. Confidentiality

### 10.1 Definition

"Confidential Information" means any non-public business, technical, financial, customer, or operational information disclosed by one party to the other in connection with this Agreement, whether orally or in writing, that is either marked as confidential or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given its nature or the circumstances of disclosure.

### 10.2 Mutual Confidentiality

Both parties agree to:

- Keep Confidential Information secret using at least the same degree of care they use to protect their own confidential information, and no less than reasonable care.
- Use Confidential Information only for purposes of performing under this Agreement.
- Return or destroy Confidential Information upon project completion or upon written request, except for (a) copies retained in routine automated backups until overwritten in the normal course of business, (b) copies retained in version control history for Work Product, and (c) copies required to be retained by law, regulation, or professional standards (including tax and accounting records).

### 10.3 Exceptions

Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:

- Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party
- Was rightfully known prior to disclosure
- Is independently developed without use of the other party's Confidential Information
- Is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or regulatory authority (provided the receiving party gives prompt notice where legally permitted)

### 10.4 Data Protection

Contractor is not a data processor for Client under this Agreement and does not process personal data of EU, UK, or other end users on Client's behalf. Client acts as the data controller for any personal data collected by, stored on, or transmitted through the Work Product after delivery, and is responsible for GDPR, CCPA, and similar privacy law compliance for its own users and visitors. If the parties later determine that a processor relationship is required, they will execute a mutually acceptable Data Processing Addendum before such processing begins.

### 10.5 Security Incidents

Each party is responsible for the security of its own systems, accounts, and devices. If either party becomes aware of a confirmed security incident affecting the other party's Confidential Information or Work Product, it will notify the other party in writing without undue delay (and no later than 72 hours after confirmation). Neither party is liable for breaches of third-party platforms (e.g., Vercel, Supabase, GitHub, email providers) except to the extent caused by that party's own negligence or willful misconduct.

## 11. Indemnification

**Contractor → Client.** Contractor will defend and indemnify Client from third-party claims that the Work Product, as delivered, infringes the intellectual-property rights of a third party, provided Client gives prompt written notice and reasonable cooperation. This indemnity excludes claims arising from (a) Open-Source Components governed by their own licenses, (b) Client-supplied materials, (c) modifications to the Work Product made after delivery by anyone other than Contractor, and (d) use of the Work Product in combination with other products or services not provided by Contractor.

**Client → Contractor.** Client will defend and indemnify Contractor against claims arising from (a) Client-supplied materials, (b) Client's breach of this Agreement, or (c) Client's use of the Work Product in violation of applicable law.

**Indemnity Cap:** Contractor's total cumulative liability under this § 11 is capped at two (2) times the total fees actually paid by Client to Contractor under this Agreement.

The party seeking indemnity must give prompt written notice and reasonable cooperation; failure to do so relieves the indemnifying party only to the extent of actual prejudice caused by the delay.

---

## 12. Limitation of Liability

### 12.1 Limitation of Damages

Except as stated in § 12.2, neither party's total cumulative liability arising out of or related to this Agreement will exceed the total fees paid by Client to Contractor under this Agreement. Neither party will be liable for:

- Indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages
- Lost profits or business opportunities
- Loss of data not caused by the liable party's negligence or willful misconduct

### 12.2 Exceptions to Liability Cap

The cap in § 12.1 does not apply to:

- A party's payment obligations under this Agreement
- A party's breach of confidentiality obligations under § 10
- A party's gross negligence or willful misconduct
- Client's liability under § 11 (Client → Contractor indemnity)

Contractor's liability for intellectual property infringement claims under § 11 is capped as stated in § 11 and is not subject to the general cap in § 12.1.

## 13. Term & Termination

- **Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and ends upon final payment and final delivery of the Work Product, unless earlier terminated.
- **Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate with seven (7) days' written notice if the other materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within that seven-day period. If Client terminates for Cause due to Contractor's uncured material breach, Client's obligation is limited to payment for work actually completed and accepted through the termination date; no kill fee or restart fee applies, and Contractor will deliver to Client all work product completed through that date.
- **Termination for Convenience by Client.** If Client terminates this Agreement for convenience (i.e., not for Contractor's uncured breach), Contractor will cease work and invoice Client for (a) all work performed through the termination date at the hourly rate in § 1.2 or, at Contractor's election, a pro-rata share of the phase in progress, plus (b) a kill fee equal to twenty percent (20%) of the total Project Investment not yet invoiced. Any Deposit previously paid will be credited first against amounts owed under this section; Client remains responsible for any remaining balance.
- **Termination for Client Breach.** If Client materially breaches this Agreement (including non-payment or sustained unresponsiveness) and fails to cure within seven (7) days of written notice, Contractor may terminate and invoice Client for the full remaining balance of the Project Investment. Any Deposit previously paid will be credited against that balance.

## 16. Dispute Resolution

The parties will first attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute by direct negotiation. If the dispute is not resolved within fifteen (15) business days of written notice, any dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association under its Commercial Arbitration Rules, held virtually, before one arbitrator. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

**Small Claims Carve-Out:** Notwithstanding the foregoing, either party may bring an individual claim in small claims court for any dispute within the jurisdictional limit of such court (generally $15,000 or less in Georgia). Claims for collection of undisputed invoiced amounts may also be brought in a court of competent jurisdiction.
